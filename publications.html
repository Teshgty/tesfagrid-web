§1. Current volumes

Volume I · Theorem 7

Theorem 7: A mod-6 constraint on consecutive prime gaps. Tesfaye Dereje. The Tesfa Grid, Volume I, 2026.

Elementary statement and proof that consecutive prime gaps between primes greater than 3 fall in residue classes 0, 2, or 4 modulo 6. Verified empirically across 348,511 consecutive gaps.

Volume II · The Column-Class Sieve

The Column-Class Sieve: a theorem-guided approach to prime generation. Tesfaye Dereje. The Tesfa Grid, Volume II, 2026.

A prime-generation algorithm derived from the structure established in Volume I. Benchmark performance measured on the Ethoryx reference implementation. Parameter choices and source code are held internally.

Volume III · The Oscillation Law

The Oscillation Law: empirical alternation in prime-gap second-differences. Tesfaye Dereje. The Tesfa Grid, Volume III, 2026.

Empirical observation that the sign of prime-gap second-differences alternates at a frequency of approximately 68.2%, against a null expectation of 50%. Z-score approximately 211 across the sample. Conjecture; proof remains open.

§3. Further volumes

Several further volumes are in preparation. The programme currently projects nine theorems in total, of which three are published above in research-note form. Future volumes will extend the framework to related structural questions in number theory and will appear on this site as they become ready.
