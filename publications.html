§1. Primary works

Volume I · Theorem 7

Theorem 7: A mod-6 constraint on consecutive prime gaps. Tesfaye Dereje. The Tesfa Grid, Volume I, 2026.

Elementary proof that consecutive prime gaps between primes greater than 3 are always congruent to 0, 2, or 4 modulo 6. Empirically verified across 348,511 consecutive gaps with zero violations.

Volume II · The Column-Class Sieve

The Column-Class Sieve: theorem-guided prime generation. Tesfaye Dereje. The Tesfa Grid, Volume II, 2026.

A prime-generation algorithm for number-theoretic transform primes. Proved 75% candidate reduction relative to naive sampling, with open-source C/GMP reference implementation.

Volume III · The Oscillation Law

The Oscillation Law: empirical alternation of consecutive prime-gap differences. Tesfaye Dereje. The Tesfa Grid, Volume III, 2026.

Empirical observation that sign of the second-difference G2(k) alternates 68.2% of the time, against a null expectation of 50%. Z = 211 across 348,511 samples. Stated as a conjecture; proof remains open.

§3. Suggested reading order

For readers new to the programme, we suggest the following sequence:

  1. The introduction on this site, for context and motivation.
  2. Volume I (Theorem 7), which establishes the mathematical foundation.
  3. Volume II (the Sieve), which converts the theorem into algorithm.
  4. Volume III (the Oscillation Law), for the current empirical frontier.

Each volume is self-contained enough to be read independently, but the later volumes assume familiarity with the notation and terminology introduced in the earlier ones.
