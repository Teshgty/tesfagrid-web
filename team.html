The researcher and the programme

§1. Principal investigator

Tesfaye Dereje

Independent mathematical researcher based in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia. Founder of the Tesfa Grid research programme and of the Ethoryx commercial arm that applies its findings to cryptography. Active in number theory since 2021, with particular interest in the additive and multiplicative structure of prime numbers and their computational consequences.

The Tesfa Grid programme began in 2023 as a personal effort to understand whether the apparent disorder of prime numbers concealed testable regularities. The first concrete result was Theorem 7, the mod-6 gap constraint, formulated in 2024 and proved in 2025. Volume II (the Column-Class Sieve) followed shortly after as a direct application. Volume III (the Oscillation Law) emerged from observation during the empirical verification of Theorem 7 at large scale, and remains open.

Research interests

  • Additive structure of prime gaps
  • Wheel sieves and column-class decompositions
  • Number-theoretic transforms and primes of special form
  • Post-quantum cryptography and lattice-friendly prime generation
  • Deterministic primality testing in constrained computational environments

§2. The commercial programme

The mathematics described on this site has a commercial sibling: Ethoryx Research, which develops cryptographic software derived from these findings. Ethoryx is operated by the same author but with a distinct focus — producing production-grade implementations, vendor integrations, and commercial partnerships.

The two are intentionally separate. This site publishes openly under the MIT License; the commercial site is a product company with its own licensing structure. What connects them is the mathematics: every result on ethoryx.io traces back to a volume on this site.

§3. The future — the Tesfa Grid Institute

A long-term goal of this programme is the establishment of the Tesfa Grid Institute, an independent mathematical research institution based in Addis Ababa. The Institute would support young Ethiopian mathematicians working in number theory and its computational applications, publish open research under its own imprint, and serve as a durable home for this and successor research programmes.

This is a future commitment, not a present fact. The Institute does not yet exist. What exists is the intention, and the mathematics being accumulated here to make its eventual founding credible.

Readers interested in supporting this long-term vision — as collaborators, benefactors, or future students — are invited to write to research@ethoryx.io.
